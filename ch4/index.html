üìö Core Definitions

  • Sequence (Progression): An ordered set of numbers, formed according to a definite rule, is called a sequence (or progression), and the individual members are called terms (or elements) of the sequence.
  • Arithmetic Sequence (A.P): A sequence in which each term is formed by adding a fixed number (common difference, \(d\)) to the preceding term.
  • Geometric Sequence (G.P): A sequence in which each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous term by a fixed, non-zero number (common ratio, \(r\)).
  • Harmonic Sequence (H.P): A sequence in which the reciprocals of its terms form an arithmetic sequence.
  • Arithmetic Mean (A.M): If \(a, A, b\) are in A.P, then \(A\) is the arithmetic mean of \(a\) and \(b\), given by \(A = \frac{a + b}{2}\).
  • Geometric Mean (G.M): If \(a, G, b\) are in G.P, then \(G\) is the geometric mean of \(a\) and \(b\), given by \(G = \pm \sqrt{ab}\).
  • Harmonic Mean (H.M): If \(a, H, b\) are in H.P, then \(H\) is the harmonic mean of \(a\) and \(b\), given by \(H = \frac{2ab}{a + b}\).
  • Triangular Sequence: A sequence where the \(n\)th term is given by \(T_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\).
  • Factorial Sequence: A sequence involving factorials, e.g., \(a_n = \frac{1}{n!}\).
  • Pascal Sequence: The terms of Pascal's triangle, where the \(n\)th row is given by \(\binom{n}{r}\) for \(r = 0, 1, \dots, n\).
  • Arithmetic Series: The sum of the terms of an arithmetic sequence.
  • Geometric Series: The sum of the terms of a geometric sequence.
  • Infinite Geometric Series: A geometric series with an infinite number of terms, convergent if \(|r| < 1\).
  • Recurring Decimal: A decimal representation of a number whose digits are periodic, e.g., \(0.\overline{3}\).

Q4. Find the values of nth term of triangular sequence when \(n = 7, 9, 12\) and \(16\).

Solution:

The general term of the triangular sequence is given by:

\(T_n = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\)

(iii) For \(n = 12\):

\(T_{12} = \frac{12(12+1)}{2} = \frac{12 \times 13}{2} = 78\)

(iv) For \(n = 16\):

\(T_{16} = \frac{16(16+1)}{2} = \frac{16 \times 17}{2} = 136\)

\(a_{21} = a + 20d = -9 + 20(4) = -9 + 80 = 71\)

Q7. The sum of \(n\) terms of two arithmetic series are in the ratio \(3n + 2 : n + 1\). Find the ratio of their 8th terms.

Solution:

Let the sums of the two series be \(S_n\) and \(S'_n\):

\(\frac{S_n}{S'_n} = \frac{3n + 2}{n + 1}\)

Using \(S_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a + (n-1)d]\) and \(S'_n = \frac{n}{2} [2a' + (n-1)d']\):

\(\frac{2a + (n-1)d}{2a' + (n-1)d'} = \frac{3n + 2}{n + 1}\)

For large \(n\), the ratio of coefficients of \(n\) gives:

\(\frac{d}{d'} = 3\)

For \(n = 1\):

\(\frac{2a}{2a'} = \frac{5}{2} \Rightarrow \frac{a}{a'} = \frac{5}{2}\)

Thus, the ratio of 8th terms:

\(\frac{a_8}{a'_8} = \frac{a + 7d}{a' + 7d'} = \frac{5 + 7 \times 3}{2 + 7 \times 1} = \frac{5 + 21}{2 + 7} = \frac{26}{9}\)

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{\frac{26}{9}}\)
