üìö Core Definitions

  • Function: A rule or correspondence that assigns each element of a set \( X \) (domain) to one and only one element of a set \( Y \) (co-domain).
  • Domain: The set of all possible input values (pre-images) for a function.
  • Co-domain: The set of all possible output values (images) for a function.
  • Range: The set of all actual output values (images) produced by a function.
  • One-to-One Function (Injective): A function where distinct elements of the domain map to distinct elements of the co-domain.
  • Onto Function (Surjective): A function where every element of the co-domain is mapped to by some element of the domain.
  • Bijective Function: A function that is both one-to-one and onto.
  • Inverse Function: For a bijective function \( f: X \to Y \), the inverse function \( f^{-1}: Y \to X \) satisfies \( f^{-1}(f(x)) = x \) and \( f(f^{-1}(y)) = y \).
  • Linear Function: A function of the form \( f(x) = ax + b \), where \( a \neq 0 \).
  • Quadratic Function: A function of the form \( f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c \), where \( a \neq 0 \).
  • Square Root Function: A function of the form \( f(x) = \sqrt{x} \), defined for \( x \geq 0 \).
  • Polynomial Function: A function of the form \( p(x) = a_nx^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \dots + a_0 \), where \( a_n \neq 0 \).

Q7. Find the domain of the following functions:
(i) \( f(x) = x^2 + 3 \)

Solution:

The function \( f(x) = x^2 + 3 \) is a polynomial function.

Polynomial functions are defined for all real numbers.

Therefore, the domain of \( f(x) \) is all real numbers, i.e., \( \mathbb{R} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{\mathbb{R}}\)

(ii) \( f(x) = \sqrt{x+3} \)

Solution:

The function \( f(x) = \sqrt{x+3} \) is defined only when the radicand is non-negative.

So, \( x + 3 \geq 0 \Rightarrow x \geq -3 \).

Therefore, the domain of \( f(x) \) is \( \{x \mid x \geq -3\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{[-3, \infty)}\)

(iii) \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x} \)

Solution:

The function \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x} \) is undefined when the denominator is zero.

So, \( x \neq 0 \).

Therefore, the domain of \( f(x) \) is \( \mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}}\)

(iv) \( f(x) = \sqrt{x-5} \)

Solution:

The function \( f(x) = \sqrt{x-5} \) is defined only when the radicand is non-negative.

So, \( x - 5 \geq 0 \Rightarrow x \geq 5 \).

Therefore, the domain of \( f(x) \) is \( \{x \mid x \geq 5\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{[5, \infty)}\)

(v) \( f(x) = \frac{x+1}{x-2} \)

Solution:

The function \( f(x) = \frac{x+1}{x-2} \) is undefined when the denominator is zero.

So, \( x - 2 \neq 0 \Rightarrow x \neq 2 \).

Therefore, the domain of \( f(x) \) is \( \mathbb{R} \setminus \{2\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{2\}}\)

(vi) \( f(x) = \frac{x^2}{x^2-9} \)

Solution:

The function \( f(x) = \frac{x^2}{x^2-9} \) is undefined when the denominator is zero.

So, \( x^2 - 9 \neq 0 \Rightarrow x \neq \pm 3 \).

Therefore, the domain of \( f(x) \) is \( \mathbb{R} \setminus \{-3, 3\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{-3, 3\}}\)

Q12. The domain of the function \( f(x) = 5x+1 \) is \(\{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}\). Find its range.

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = 5x + 1 \) and domain \( = \{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5\} \).

Compute \( f(x) \) for each \( x \):

\( f(0) = 5(0) + 1 = 1 \)

\( f(1) = 5(1) + 1 = 6 \)

\( f(2) = 5(2) + 1 = 11 \)

\( f(3) = 5(3) + 1 = 16 \)

\( f(4) = 5(4) + 1 = 21 \)

\( f(5) = 5(5) + 1 = 26 \)

Therefore, the range of \( f(x) \) is \( \{1, 6, 11, 16, 21, 26\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{\{1, 6, 11, 16, 21, 26\}}\)

Q13. If \( f(x) = x^3 - ax^2 + bx + 1 \). Find the values of \( a \) and \( b \), where \( f(2) = -3 \) and \( f(-1) = 0 \).

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = x^3 - ax^2 + bx + 1 \), \( f(2) = -3 \), and \( f(-1) = 0 \).

Substitute \( x = 2 \):

\( f(2) = 8 - 4a + 2b + 1 = -3 \Rightarrow 9 - 4a + 2b = -3 \Rightarrow 4a - 2b = 12 \Rightarrow 2a - b = 6 \quad \text{(1)} \)

Substitute \( x = -1 \):

\( f(-1) = -1 - a - b + 1 = 0 \Rightarrow -a - b = 0 \Rightarrow a + b = 0 \quad \text{(2)} \)

Solve (1) and (2):

From (2): \( b = -a \).

Substitute in (1): \( 2a - (-a) = 6 \Rightarrow 3a = 6 \Rightarrow a = 2 \).

Then \( b = -2 \).

‚úÖ Answer: \( a = \boxed{2} \), \( b = \boxed{-2} \)

Q1(i). If \( f(x) = -5x + 1 \), find the value of \( f^{-1}(36) \).

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = -5x + 1 \).

Let \( y = f(x) \Rightarrow y = -5x + 1 \).

Solve for \( x \):

\( y = -5x + 1 \Rightarrow y - 1 = -5x \Rightarrow x = \frac{1 - y}{5} \).

Thus, \( f^{-1}(y) = \frac{1 - y}{5} \).

Now, \( f^{-1}(36) = \frac{1 - 36}{5} = \frac{-35}{5} = -7 \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{-7}\)

Q2(ii). Given that \( g(t) = \frac{1}{t-5}, (t \neq 5) \), find the value of \( g^{-1}(2) \).

Solution:

Given \( g(t) = \frac{1}{t-5} \).

Let \( y = g(t) \Rightarrow y = \frac{1}{t-5} \).

Solve for \( t \):

\( y = \frac{1}{t-5} \Rightarrow t - 5 = \frac{1}{y} \Rightarrow t = 5 + \frac{1}{y} \).

Thus, \( g^{-1}(y) = 5 + \frac{1}{y} \).

Now, \( g^{-1}(2) = 5 + \frac{1}{2} = 5.5 \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{5.5}\)

Q3(iii). Find the inverse of the function \( f(x) = \frac{2x+1}{5} \).

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = \frac{2x+1}{5} \).

Let \( y = f(x) \Rightarrow y = \frac{2x+1}{5} \).

Solve for \( x \):

\( 5y = 2x + 1 \Rightarrow 5y - 1 = 2x \Rightarrow x = \frac{5y - 1}{2} \).

Thus, \( f^{-1}(y) = \frac{5y - 1}{2} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{f^{-1}(x) = \frac{5x - 1}{2}}\)

Q4(i). Find inverse of \( f(x) = \frac{x-1}{x-3}, x \neq 3 \) and determine the domain and range of \( f^{-1} \).

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = \frac{x-1}{x-3} \).

Let \( y = f(x) \Rightarrow y = \frac{x-1}{x-3} \).

Solve for \( x \):

\( y(x-3) = x-1 \Rightarrow yx - 3y = x - 1 \Rightarrow yx - x = 3y - 1 \Rightarrow x(y-1) = 3y - 1 \Rightarrow x = \frac{3y - 1}{y - 1} \).

Thus, \( f^{-1}(y) = \frac{3y - 1}{y - 1} \).

Domain of \( f^{-1} \): \( y \neq 1 \).

Range of \( f^{-1} \): \( \mathbb{R} \setminus \{3\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \( f^{-1}(x) = \boxed{\frac{3x - 1}{x - 1}} \), Domain: \( \boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{1\}} \), Range: \( \boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{3\}} \)

Q4(ii). Find inverse of \( f(x) = 5x+7 \) and determine the domain and range of \( f^{-1} \).

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = 5x + 7 \).

Let \( y = f(x) \Rightarrow y = 5x + 7 \).

Solve for \( x \):

\( y = 5x + 7 \Rightarrow y - 7 = 5x \Rightarrow x = \frac{y - 7}{5} \).

Thus, \( f^{-1}(y) = \frac{y - 7}{5} \).

Domain of \( f^{-1} \): \( \mathbb{R} \).

Range of \( f^{-1} \): \( \mathbb{R} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \( f^{-1}(x) = \boxed{\frac{x - 7}{5}} \), Domain: \( \boxed{\mathbb{R}} \), Range: \( \boxed{\mathbb{R}} \)

Q4(iii). Find inverse of \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x+5}, x \neq -5 \) and determine the domain and range of \( f^{-1} \).

Solution:

Given \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x+5} \).

Let \( y = f(x) \Rightarrow y = \frac{1}{x+5} \).

Solve for \( x \):

\( y = \frac{1}{x+5} \Rightarrow x + 5 = \frac{1}{y} \Rightarrow x = \frac{1}{y} - 5 \).

Thus, \( f^{-1}(y) = \frac{1}{y} - 5 \).

Domain of \( f^{-1} \): \( y \neq 0 \).

Range of \( f^{-1} \): \( \mathbb{R} \setminus \{-5\} \).

‚úÖ Answer: \( f^{-1}(x) = \boxed{\frac{1}{x} - 5} \), Domain: \( \boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}} \), Range: \( \boxed{\mathbb{R} \setminus \{-5\}} \)

Q3. Find the point of intersection of the following functions graphically:
(i) \( f(x) = x + 2 \) and \( g(x) = x^2 - 4x + 6 \)

Solution:

To find the points of intersection, solve \( f(x) = g(x) \):

\( x + 2 = x^2 - 4x + 6 \)

\( x^2 - 5x + 4 = 0 \)

\( (x - 1)(x - 4) = 0 \)

Thus, \( x = 1 \) or \( x = 4 \).

Compute \( y \) for each \( x \):

For \( x = 1 \): \( y = 1 + 2 = 3 \)

For \( x = 4 \): \( y = 4 + 2 = 6 \)

Therefore, the points of intersection are \( (1, 3) \) and \( (4, 6) \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{(1, 3)}\) and \(\boxed{(4, 6)}\)

(ii) \( f(x) = x + 4 \) and \( g(x) = x^2 - 6x + 10 \)

Solution:

To find the points of intersection, solve \( f(x) = g(x) \):

\( x + 4 = x^2 - 6x + 10 \)

\( x^2 - 7x + 6 = 0 \)

\( (x - 1)(x - 6) = 0 \)

Thus, \( x = 1 \) or \( x = 6 \).

Compute \( y \) for each \( x \):

For \( x = 1 \): \( y = 1 + 4 = 5 \)

For \( x = 6 \): \( y = 6 + 4 = 10 \)

Therefore, the points of intersection are \( (1, 5) \) and \( (6, 10) \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{(1, 5)}\) and \(\boxed{(6, 10)}\)

Q4. The paths of two aeroplanes A and B in the plane are determined by the straight lines \( 2x - y = 6 \) and \( 3x + y = 4 \) respectively. Graphically find the point where the two paths cross each other.

Solution:

Solve the system of equations:

\( 2x - y = 6 \) and \( 3x + y = 4 \).

Add the two equations:

\( 5x = 10 \Rightarrow x = 2 \).

Substitute \( x = 2 \) into \( 2x - y = 6 \):

\( 4 - y = 6 \Rightarrow y = -2 \).

Therefore, the point of intersection is \( (2, -2) \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{(2, -2)}\)

Q5. If the sum of two numbers is 6 and the square of the first number is greater than 6 by the second number. Solve graphically and find the numbers.

Solution:

Let the numbers be \( x \) and \( y \).

Given:

\( x + y = 6 \) and \( x^2 = y + 6 \).

From the first equation: \( y = 6 - x \).

Substitute into the second equation:

\( x^2 = (6 - x) + 6 \Rightarrow x^2 = 12 - x \Rightarrow x^2 + x - 12 = 0 \).

Solve the quadratic equation:

\( x = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{1 + 48}}{2} = \frac{-1 \pm 7}{2} \).

Thus, \( x = 3 \) or \( x = -4 \).

For \( x = 3 \): \( y = 3 \).

For \( x = -4 \): \( y = 10 \).

Therefore, the numbers are \( 3 \) and \( 3 \), or \( -4 \) and \( 10 \).

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{3, 3}\) or \(\boxed{-4, 10}\)
