üìö Core Definitions

  • Matrix: A rectangular array of numbers, symbols, or expressions arranged in rows and columns.
  • Order of a Matrix: A matrix with \( m \) rows and \( n \) columns has order \( m \times n \).
  • Square Matrix: A matrix with equal number of rows and columns (\( m = n \)).
  • Diagonal Matrix: A square matrix where all non-diagonal elements are zero.
  • Scalar Matrix: A diagonal matrix where all diagonal elements are equal.
  • Identity Matrix: A diagonal matrix where all diagonal elements are 1, denoted by \( I_n \).
  • Symmetric Matrix: A square matrix where \( A^t = A \).
  • Skew-Symmetric Matrix: A square matrix where \( A^t = -A \).
  • Singular Matrix: A square matrix whose determinant is zero.
  • Non-Singular Matrix: A square matrix whose determinant is non-zero.
  • Adjoint of a Matrix: Transpose of the matrix of cofactors, denoted by \( \text{adj } A \).
  • Rank of a Matrix: Number of non-zero rows in echelon form of a matrix.
  • Determinant: A scalar value associated with a square matrix.
  • Cofactor: \( A_{ij} = (-1)^{i+j} M_{ij} \), where \( M_{ij} \) is the minor.
  • Echelon Form: A matrix form where leading entry of each row is 1 and all entries below it are zero.

Q6. Solve the non-homogeneous system using Cramer's Rule:

(i) \( \begin{array}{l} x - 2y + z = 2 \\ 2x + 2y - z = 4 \\ x + y + 0.z = 0 \end{array} \)

Step 1: Write in matrix form:

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}, \quad B = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

Step 2: Find \( |A| \):

\[ |A| = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1 \\ 2 & 2 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 \end{vmatrix} \] \[ = 1(0 + 1) + 2(0 + 1) + 1(2 - 2) = 1 + 2 + 0 = 3 \]

Step 3: Find \( |A_x| \) (replace first column with B):

\[ |A_x| = \begin{vmatrix} 2 & -2 & 1 \\ 4 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{vmatrix} \]

Expand by third row:

\[ = -1 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 4 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = -1(-2 - 4) = 6 \]

Step 4: Find \( |A_y| \) (replace second column with B):

\[ |A_y| = \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{vmatrix} \]

Expand by third row:

\[ = 1 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 4 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = 1(-2 - 4) = -6 \]

Step 5: Find \( |A_z| \) (replace third column with B):
