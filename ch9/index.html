Chapter 9: Linear Programming (LP)

Unit 9: Linear Programming ‚Äì Complete Solved Notes

üìö Core Definitions

  • Linear Programming (LP): A mathematical technique for allocating limited resources in an optimal manner, used to solve complex planning problems involving constraints and objectives.
  • Linear Inequality: An inequality involving a linear expression in one or more variables, e.g., \( ax + b < c \), \( ax + by \geq c \).
  • Feasible Region: The set of all possible points that satisfy all constraints of an LP problem, including non-negativity constraints.
  • Objective Function: A linear function that expresses the main aim of an LP problem, either to be maximized or minimized.
  • Problem Constraints: Linear inequalities or equations that define limitations on the decision variables in an LP problem.
  • Decision Variables: The unknowns in an LP problem, representing quantities to be determined, e.g., \( x, y \).
  • Optimal Solution: The feasible solution that maximizes or minimizes the objective function in an LP problem.
  • Corner Points: The vertices of the feasible region, where the optimal solution is found.
  • Graphical Method: A method to solve LP problems by graphing constraints and identifying the feasible region and corner points.
  • Non-Negativity Constraints: Constraints that restrict decision variables to non-negative values, e.g., \( x \geq 0, y \geq 0 \).

Q1(i). Draw the graph of the following system of linear inequalities and identify the feasible region and the corner points: \( 2x - 3y \leq 8 \), \( 3x + 2y \leq 10 \), \( x \geq 0, y \geq 0 \).

Solution:

Step 1: Convert inequalities to equations: \( 2x - 3y = 8 \), \( 3x + 2y = 10 \).

Step 2: Find intercepts: For \( 2x - 3y = 8 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (4, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, -\frac{8}{3}) \). For \( 3x + 2y = 10 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (\frac{10}{3}, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 5) \).

Step 3: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the two lines.

Step 4: Find corner points: Intersection of \( 2x - 3y = 8 \) and \( 3x + 2y = 10 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 4x - 6y = 16 \), \( 9x + 6y = 30 \). Adding: \( 13x = 46 \implies x = \frac{46}{13} \), \( y = \frac{2}{13} \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (4, 0) \), \( (\frac{46}{13}, \frac{2}{13}) \), \( (0, 5) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Corner points are \(\boxed{(0, 0)}\), \(\boxed{(4, 0)}\), \(\boxed{\left(\frac{46}{13}, \frac{2}{13}\right)}\), and \(\boxed{(0, 5)}\).

Q2(i). Draw the graph of the following inequalities and also identify the solution space and the corner points: \( 5x - y \leq 16 \), \( 3x + 2y \leq 6 \), \( x + 2y \leq 5 \), \( x \geq 0, y \geq 0 \).

Solution:

Step 1: Convert inequalities to equations: \( 5x - y = 16 \), \( 3x + 2y = 6 \), \( x + 2y = 5 \).

Step 2: Find intercepts: For \( 5x - y = 16 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (3.2, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, -16) \). For \( 3x + 2y = 6 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (2, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 3) \). For \( x + 2y = 5 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (5, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 2.5) \).

Step 3: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the three lines.

Step 4: Find corner points: Intersection of \( 5x - y = 16 \) and \( 3x + 2y = 6 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 10x - 2y = 32 \), \( 3x + 2y = 6 \). Adding: \( 13x = 38 \implies x = \frac{38}{13} \), \( y = -\frac{104}{13} \). Intersection of \( 3x + 2y = 6 \) and \( x + 2y = 5 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 3x + 2y = 6 \), \( x + 2y = 5 \). Subtract: \( 2x = 1 \implies x = 0.5 \), \( y = 2.25 \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (3.2, 0) \), \( (0.5, 2.25) \), \( (0, 2.5) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Corner points are \(\boxed{(0, 0)}\), \(\boxed{(3.2, 0)}\), \(\boxed{(0.5, 2.25)}\), and \(\boxed{(0, 2.5)}\).

Q1(i). Maximize \( Z(x, y) = 10x + 11y \) subject to: \( 2x + 3y \leq 8 \), \( 6x + 3y \leq 10 \), \( x, y \geq 0 \).

Solution:

Step 1: Convert inequalities to equations: \( 2x + 3y = 8 \), \( 6x + 3y = 10 \).

Step 2: Find intercepts: For \( 2x + 3y = 8 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (4, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, \frac{8}{3}) \). For \( 6x + 3y = 10 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (\frac{5}{3}, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, \frac{10}{3}) \).

Step 3: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the two lines.

Step 4: Find corner points: Intersection of \( 2x + 3y = 8 \) and \( 6x + 3y = 10 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 2x + 3y = 8 \), \( 6x + 3y = 10 \). Subtract first from second: \( 4x = 2 \implies x = 0.5 \), \( y = \frac{7}{3} \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (4, 0) \), \( (0.5, \frac{7}{3}) \), \( (0, \frac{8}{3}) \).

Step 5: Evaluate \( Z \) at corner points: \( Z(0, 0) = 0 \), \( Z(4, 0) = 40 \), \( Z(0.5, \frac{7}{3}) = 5 + \frac{77}{3} = \frac{92}{3} \approx 30.67 \), \( Z(0, \frac{8}{3}) = \frac{88}{3} \approx 29.33 \).

Step 6: The maximum value of \( Z \) is \( \frac{92}{3} \) at \( (0.5, \frac{7}{3}) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Maximum \( Z = \boxed{\frac{92}{3}} \) at \( \boxed{\left(0.5, \frac{7}{3}\right)} \).

Q1(ii). Maximize \( Z(x, y) = 30x + 36y \) subject to: \( 4x + 2y \leq 12 \), \( 6x + 5y \leq 20 \), \( x, y \geq 0 \).

Solution:

Step 1: Convert inequalities to equations: \( 4x + 2y = 12 \), \( 6x + 5y = 20 \).

Step 2: Find intercepts: For \( 4x + 2y = 12 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (3, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 6) \). For \( 6x + 5y = 20 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (\frac{10}{3}, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 4) \).

Step 3: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the two lines.

Step 4: Find corner points: Intersection of \( 4x + 2y = 12 \) and \( 6x + 5y = 20 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 4x + 2y = 12 \), \( 6x + 5y = 20 \). Multiply first by 3: \( 12x + 6y = 36 \). Multiply second by 2: \( 12x + 10y = 40 \). Subtract: \( -4y = -4 \implies y = 1 \), \( x = 2.5 \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (3, 0) \), \( (2.5, 1) \), \( (0, 4) \).

Step 5: Evaluate \( Z \) at corner points: \( Z(0, 0) = 0 \), \( Z(3, 0) = 90 \), \( Z(2.5, 1) = 75 + 36 = 111 \), \( Z(0, 4) = 144 \).

Step 6: The maximum value of \( Z \) is \( 144 \) at \( (0, 4) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Maximum \( Z = \boxed{144} \) at \( \boxed{(0, 4)} \).

Step 3: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the three lines.

Subtract first from second: \( 2x = 40 \implies x = 20 \), \( y = 30 \). Intersection of \( 3x + y = 90 \) and \( x - y = 40 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 3x + y = 90 \), \( x - y = 40 \). Add: \( 4x = 130 \implies x = 32.5 \), \( y = -8.5 \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (40, 0) \), \( (32.5, -8.5) \), \( (20, 30) \), \( (0, 40) \).

Step 5: Evaluate \( Z \) at corner points: \( Z(0, 0) = 0 \), \( Z(40, 0) = 160 \), \( Z(32.5, -8.5) = 130 - 8.5 = 121.5 \), \( Z(20, 30) = 80 + 30 = 110 \), \( Z(0, 40) = 40 \).

Q2. A landlord has 50 hectares of land to grow two crops X and Y with profits per hectare estimated as Rs 10,500 and Rs 9,000 respectively. To control weeds, a liquid herbicide no more than 800 litres is to be used for crops X and Y at rates of 20 liters and 10 liters per hectare. How much land should be allocated to each crop so as to maximize the total profit?

Solution:

Step 1: Define variables: Let \( x \) = hectares for crop X, \( y \) = hectares for crop Y.

Step 2: Formulate LP problem: Maximize \( Z = 10500x + 9000y \) Subject to: \( x + y \leq 50 \), \( 20x + 10y \leq 800 \), \( x, y \geq 0 \).

Step 3: Convert inequalities to equations: \( x + y = 50 \), \( 20x + 10y = 800 \).

Step 4: Find intercepts: For \( x + y = 50 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (50, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 50) \). For \( 20x + 10y = 800 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (40, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 80) \).

Step 5: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the two lines.

Step 6: Find corner points: Intersection of \( x + y = 50 \) and \( 20x + 10y = 800 \): Solve simultaneously: \( x + y = 50 \), \( 2x + y = 80 \). Subtract first from second: \( x = 30 \), \( y = 20 \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (40, 0) \), \( (30, 20) \), \( (0, 50) \).

Step 7: Evaluate \( Z \) at corner points: \( Z(0, 0) = 0 \), \( Z(40, 0) = 420000 \), \( Z(30, 20) = 315000 + 180000 = 495000 \), \( Z(0, 50) = 450000 \).

Step 8: The maximum profit is Rs 495,000 at \( (30, 20) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Allocate \(\boxed{30 \text{ hectares}}\) to crop X and \(\boxed{20 \text{ hectares}}\) to crop Y for maximum profit of \(\boxed{\text{Rs } 495,000}\).

Q3. A toy factory manufactures two types of toys A and B and sells for Rs.25 and Rs.20 respectively. The toys A and B respectively, require 20 units and 12 units daily out of 2000 available resource units. Both require a production time of 5 minutes and total working hours are 9 per day. What should be the quantity for each of the toys to maximize the selling amount?

Solution:

Step 1: Define variables: Let \( x \) = number of toys A, \( y \) = number of toys B.

Step 2: Formulate LP problem: Maximize \( Z = 25x + 20y \) Subject to: \( 20x + 12y \leq 2000 \), \( 5x + 5y \leq 540 \), \( x, y \geq 0 \).

Step 3: Convert inequalities to equations: \( 20x + 12y = 2000 \), \( 5x + 5y = 540 \).

Step 4: Find intercepts: For \( 20x + 12y = 2000 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (100, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, \frac{500}{3}) \). For \( 5x + 5y = 540 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (108, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 108) \).

Step 5: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the axes and the intersection of the two lines.

Step 6: Find corner points: Intersection of \( 20x + 12y = 2000 \) and \( 5x + 5y = 540 \): Solve simultaneously: \( 20x + 12y = 2000 \), \( x + y = 108 \). Multiply second by 12: \( 12x + 12y = 1296 \). Subtract from first: \( 8x = 704 \implies x = 88 \), \( y = 20 \). Corner points: \( (0, 0) \), \( (100, 0) \), \( (88, 20) \), \( (0, 100) \).

Step 7: Evaluate \( Z \) at corner points: \( Z(0, 0) = 0 \), \( Z(100, 0) = 2500 \), \( Z(88, 20) = 2200 + 400 = 2600 \), \( Z(0, 100) = 2000 \).

Step 8: The maximum selling amount is Rs 2600 at \( (88, 20) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Manufacture \(\boxed{88}\) toys A and \(\boxed{20}\) toys B for maximum selling amount of \(\boxed{\text{Rs } 2600}\).

Q4. At a university, Professor Abdul Sattar wishes to employ two people, Farhan and Sarfaraz, to grade papers for his classes. Farhan is a graduate student and can grade 20 papers per hour; Farhan earns \$15 per hour for grading papers. Sarfaraz is a post-doctoral associate and can grade 30 papers per hour; Sarfaraz earns \$25 per hour for grading papers. Each must be employed at least one hour a week to justify their employment. If Prof. Abdul Sattar has at least 110 papers to be graded each week, how many hours per week should he employ each person to minimize the cost?

Solution:

Step 1: Define variables: Let \( x \) = hours for Farhan, \( y \) = hours for Sarfaraz.

Step 2: Formulate LP problem: Minimize \( Z = 15x + 25y \) Subject to: \( 20x + 30y \geq 110 \), \( x \geq 1 \), \( y \geq 1 \).

Step 3: Convert inequalities to equations: \( 20x + 30y = 110 \), \( x = 1 \), \( y = 1 \).

Step 4: Find intercepts: For \( 20x + 30y = 110 \): \( x \)-intercept: \( (5.5, 0) \), \( y \)-intercept: \( (0, 3.67) \).

Step 5: Plot lines and shade feasible region: The feasible region is bounded by the lines and the axes.

Step 6: Find corner points: Intersection of \( 20x + 30y = 110 \) and \( x = 1 \): \( 20(1) + 30y = 110 \implies y = 3 \). Intersection of \( 20x + 30y = 110 \) and \( y = 1 \): \( 20x + 30(1) = 110 \implies x = 4 \). Corner points: \( (1, 1) \), \( (1, 3) \), \( (4, 1) \).

Step 7: Evaluate \( Z \) at corner points: \( Z(1, 1) = 15 + 25 = 40 \), \( Z(1, 3) = 15 + 75 = 90 \), \( Z(4, 1) = 60 + 25 = 85 \).

Step 8: The minimum cost is \$40 at \( (1, 1) \).

‚úÖ Answer: Employ Farhan for \(\boxed{1 \text{ hour}}\) and Sarfaraz for \(\boxed{1 \text{ hour}}\) to minimize cost to \(\boxed{\$40}\).
