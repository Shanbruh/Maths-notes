Vectors

Unit 3: Vectors in Plane and Space ‚Äì Complete Solved Notes

üìö Core Definitions

  • Vector: A quantity that has both magnitude and direction (e.g., force, velocity).
  • Scalar: A quantity that has only magnitude (e.g., mass, energy, area).
  • Magnitude of a Vector: The length or size of the vector, denoted \(|\vec{v}|\).
  • Unit Vector: A vector of magnitude 1; denoted \(\hat{v} = \frac{\vec{v}}{|\vec{v}|}\).
  • Zero/Null Vector: A vector with zero magnitude; initial and terminal points coincide.
  • Position Vector: Vector from origin O to point P, denoted \(\vec{OP}\).
  • Parallel Vectors: Vectors with same or opposite direction; \(\vec{b} = k\vec{a}\), \(k \in \mathbb{R}\).
  • Dot (Scalar) Product: \(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta\); yields scalar.
  • Cross (Vector) Product: \(\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta\,\hat{n}\); yields vector perpendicular to both.
  • Scalar Triple Product: \([\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})\); volume of parallelepiped.
  • Coplanar Vectors: Three vectors lying in the same plane; scalar triple product is zero.
  • Projection of \(\vec{a}\) on \(\vec{b}\): \(\text{proj}_{\vec{b}} \vec{a} = \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{b}|}\).
  • Direction Cosines: \(\cos\alpha = \frac{x}{r}, \cos\beta = \frac{y}{r}, \cos\gamma = \frac{z}{r}\); satisfy \(\cos^2\alpha + \cos^2\beta + \cos^2\gamma = 1\).
  • Polygon Law of Addition: If vectors form consecutive sides of a polygon, their sum is the closing side in opposite order.
  • Moment of Force: Torque \(\vec{M} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F}\), where \(\vec{r}\) is position vector from pivot to point of force application.

Q9. Prove the Hexagon law of vector addition.

Statement: If five vectors are represented by five consecutive sides of a hexagon taken in order, then the sixth side taken in opposite order represents their resultant.

Proof: Let hexagon be ABCDEF with vectors:

\(\vec{AB} = \vec{a},\ \vec{BC} = \vec{b},\ \vec{CD} = \vec{c},\ \vec{DE} = \vec{d},\ \vec{EF} = \vec{e}\)

We must show: \(\vec{FA} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e}\)

Using triangle law repeatedly:

\(\vec{AC} = \vec{a} + \vec{b}\)

\(\vec{AD} = \vec{AC} + \vec{c} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}\)

\(\vec{AE} = \vec{AD} + \vec{d} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d}\)

\(\vec{AF} = \vec{AE} + \vec{e} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e}\)

But \(\vec{FA} = -\vec{AF} = -(\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e})\)

However, by polygon law, the sum of all six sides is zero:

\(\vec{AB} + \vec{BC} + \vec{CD} + \vec{DE} + \vec{EF} + \vec{FA} = \vec{0}\)

\(\Rightarrow \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e} + \vec{FA} = \vec{0}\)

\(\Rightarrow \vec{FA} = -(\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e})\)

Hence, the resultant of first five is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction to the sixth side. ‚úì

Q13. Given points A(1,1), B(5,4), C(8,9), D(0,3). Show ABCD is a trapezium.

A trapezium has at least one pair of parallel sides.

Find vectors:

\(\vec{AB} = (5-1)\hat{i} + (4-1)\hat{j} = 4\hat{i} + 3\hat{j}\)

\(\vec{DC} = (8-0)\hat{i} + (9-3)\hat{j} = 8\hat{i} + 6\hat{j} = 2(4\hat{i} + 3\hat{j}) = 2\vec{AB}\)

Since \(\vec{DC} = 2\vec{AB}\), they are parallel.

Thus, AB ‚à• DC ‚áí ABCD is a trapezium. ‚úì

Q14. A, B, C have position vectors \(\vec{a}, \vec{b}, 2\vec{a} - \vec{b}\). D divides AC in 2:3 and E divides BD in 4:1. Find position vector of E.

Step 1: Position vector of C: \(\vec{c} = 2\vec{a} - \vec{b}\)

Step 2: D divides AC internally in ratio 2:3 ‚áí

\(\vec{d} = \dfrac{3\vec{a} + 2\vec{c}}{2 + 3} = \dfrac{3\vec{a} + 2(2\vec{a} - \vec{b})}{5} = \dfrac{3\vec{a} + 4\vec{a} - 2\vec{b}}{5} = \dfrac{7\vec{a} - 2\vec{b}}{5}\)

Step 3: E divides BD in ratio 4:1 (B to D) ‚áí

\(\vec{e} = \dfrac{1 \cdot \vec{b} + 4 \cdot \vec{d}}{4 + 1} = \dfrac{\vec{b} + 4\left(\dfrac{7\vec{a} - 2\vec{b}}{5}\right)}{5} = \dfrac{5\vec{b} + 28\vec{a} - 8\vec{b}}{25} = \dfrac{28\vec{a} - 3\vec{b}}{25}\)

‚úÖ Final answer: \(\boxed{\vec{e} = \dfrac{28\vec{a} - 3\vec{b}}{25}}\)

Q15. In parallelogram ABCD, X is midpoint of AB, Y divides BC in 1:2. Show that if Z divides DX in 6:1, it also divides AY in 3:4.

Assign position vectors w.r.t. A as origin:

Let \(\vec{A} = \vec{0},\ \vec{B} = \vec{b},\ \vec{D} = \vec{d} \Rightarrow \vec{C} = \vec{b} + \vec{d}\)

X = midpoint of AB ‚áí \(\vec{x} = \dfrac{\vec{0} + \vec{b}}{2} = \dfrac{\vec{b}}{2}\)

Y divides BC in 1:2 ‚áí \(\vec{y} = \dfrac{2\vec{b} + 1(\vec{b} + \vec{d})}{3} = \dfrac{3\vec{b} + \vec{d}}{3} = \vec{b} + \dfrac{\vec{d}}{3}\)

Now, find Z dividing DX in 6:1 (D to X):

\(\vec{z_1} = \dfrac{1 \cdot \vec{d} + 6 \cdot \dfrac{\vec{b}}{2}}{7} = \dfrac{\vec{d} + 3\vec{b}}{7}\)

Now, find Z dividing AY in 3:4 (A to Y):

\(\vec{z_2} = \dfrac{4 \cdot \vec{0} + 3 \cdot \left(\vec{b} + \dfrac{\vec{d}}{3}\right)}{7} = \dfrac{3\vec{b} + \vec{d}}{7}\)

Since \(\vec{z_1} = \vec{z_2}\), Z is the same point. Hence proved. ‚úì

Q7. Show that \(\sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma = 2\) for direction angles.

We know: \(\cos^2\alpha + \cos^2\beta + \cos^2\gamma = 1\)

Use identity: \(\sin^2\theta = 1 - \cos^2\theta\)

So,

\(\sin^2\alpha + \sin^2\beta + \sin^2\gamma = (1 - \cos^2\alpha) + (1 - \cos^2\beta) + (1 - \cos^2\gamma)\)

= \(3 - (\cos^2\alpha + \cos^2\beta + \cos^2\gamma) = 3 - 1 = 2\)

‚úÖ Hence proved.

Q8. Two direction angles are 45¬∞ and 60¬∞. Find the third.

\(\cos^2 45^\circ + \cos^2 60^\circ + \cos^2\gamma = 1\)

\(\left(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 + \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^2 + \cos^2\gamma = 1\)

\(\dfrac{1}{2} + \dfrac{1}{4} + \cos^2\gamma = 1 \Rightarrow \cos^2\gamma = \dfrac{1}{4}\)

\(\cos\gamma = \pm \dfrac{1}{2} \Rightarrow \gamma = 60^\circ \text{ or } 120^\circ\)

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{60^\circ \text{ or } 120^\circ}\)

Q9. Force \(\vec{F} = 7\hat{i} + 9\hat{j} - 11\hat{k}\) moves object from (4,2,7) to (6,4,9). Find work done.

Displacement \(\vec{d} = (6-4)\hat{i} + (4-2)\hat{j} + (9-7)\hat{k} = 2\hat{i} + 2\hat{j} + 2\hat{k}\)

Work \(W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} = (7)(2) + (9)(2) + (-11)(2) = 14 + 18 - 22 = 10\)

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{10 \text{ Joules}}\)

Q7. Verify \(\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} + \vec{c}) = \vec{a} \times \vec{b} + \vec{a} \times \vec{c}\) for given vectors.

Given: \(\vec{a} = 2\hat{i} + \hat{j} + 7\hat{k},\ \vec{b} = \hat{i} - 3\hat{j} + 5\hat{k},\ \vec{c} = -\hat{i} + 5\hat{j} + \hat{k}\)

\(\vec{a} \times (\vec{b} + \vec{c}) = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & 1 & 7 \\ 0 & 2 & 6 \end{vmatrix} = \hat{i}(6 - 14) - \hat{j}(12 - 0) + \hat{k}(4 - 0) = -8\hat{i} -12\hat{j} + 4\hat{k}\)

RHS: \(\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & 1 & 7 \\ 1 & -3 & 5 \end{vmatrix} = \hat{i}(5 + 21) - \hat{j}(10 - 7) + \hat{k}(-6 - 1) = 26\hat{i} -3\hat{j} -7\hat{k}\)

\(\vec{a} \times \vec{c} = \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 2 & 1 & 7 \\ -1 & 5 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = \hat{i}(1 - 35) - \hat{j}(2 + 7) + \hat{k}(10 + 1) = -34\hat{i} -9\hat{j} +11\hat{k}\)

Sum = \((26 - 34)\hat{i} + (-3 -9)\hat{j} + (-7 + 11)\hat{k} = -8\hat{i} -12\hat{j} + 4\hat{k} = \text{LHS}\)

‚úÖ Verified.

\(\begin{vmatrix} 4 & -1 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = 4(-4 + 1) + 1(6 + 1) + 1(3 + 2) = 4(-3) + 7 + 5 = -12 + 12 = 0\)

Since STP = 0, vectors are coplanar. ‚úì

Q6. Find \(\lambda\) if \(\hat{i} + \hat{j} + 2\hat{k}, \lambda\hat{i} - \hat{j} + \hat{k}, 3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} - \hat{k}\) are coplanar.

Set determinant = 0:

\(\begin{vmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 \\ \lambda & -1 & 1 \\ 3 & -2 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = 0\)

Expand: \(1(1 + 2) - 1(-\lambda - 3) + 2(-2\lambda + 3) = 3 + \lambda + 3 -4\lambda + 6 = 12 - 3\lambda = 0\)

\(\Rightarrow \lambda = 4\)

‚úÖ Answer: \(\boxed{4}\)

Long Q1. Prove the Hexagon law of vector addition.

Statement: If five vectors are represented by five consecutive sides of a hexagon taken in order, then their resultant is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction to the sixth side.

Proof: Let ABCDEF be a hexagon with vectors:

\(\vec{AB} = \vec{a},\ \vec{BC} = \vec{b},\ \vec{CD} = \vec{c},\ \vec{DE} = \vec{d},\ \vec{EF} = \vec{e}\)

We aim to show: \(\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e} = -\vec{FA}\)

Using the triangle law repeatedly:

\(\vec{AC} = \vec{a} + \vec{b}\)

\(\vec{AD} = \vec{AC} + \vec{c} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}\)

\(\vec{AE} = \vec{AD} + \vec{d} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d}\)

\(\vec{AF} = \vec{AE} + \vec{e} = \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e}\)

But \(\vec{FA} = -\vec{AF}\), so:

\(\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e} = -\vec{FA}\)

Alternatively, by the polygon law of vector addition, the sum of all six vectors forming a closed polygon is zero:

\(\vec{AB} + \vec{BC} + \vec{CD} + \vec{DE} + \vec{EF} + \vec{FA} = \vec{0}\)

\(\Rightarrow \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e} + \vec{FA} = \vec{0}\)

\(\Rightarrow \vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c} + \vec{d} + \vec{e} = -\vec{FA}\)

Hence, the resultant of the first five sides equals the sixth side in magnitude but opposite in direction. ‚úì

Long Q2. Find the volume of a tetrahedron given three co-terminal edges as vectors \(\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}\).

Formula: The volume \(V\) of a tetrahedron with co-terminal edges \(\vec{a}, \vec{b}, \vec{c}\) is:

\[ V = \frac{1}{6} \left| \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \right| = \frac{1}{6} \left| [\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] \right| \]

Derivation:

Consider a tetrahedron OABC with \(\vec{OA} = \vec{a},\ \vec{OB} = \vec{b},\ \vec{OC} = \vec{c}\).

- Area of base triangle \(OBC = \dfrac{1}{2} |\vec{b} \times \vec{c}|\)

- Height from A to plane OBC is the component of \(\vec{a}\) along the normal to the base, i.e., \(|\vec{a}| \cos\theta\), where \(\theta\) is angle between \(\vec{a}\) and \(\vec{b} \times \vec{c}\)

- So, height \(h = \dfrac{|\vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})|}{|\vec{b} \times \vec{c}|}\)

Now, volume of tetrahedron = \(\dfrac{1}{3} \times \text{Base Area} \times \text{Height}\)

\[ V = \frac{1}{3} \left( \frac{1}{2} |\vec{b} \times \vec{c}| \right) \left( \frac{|\vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})|}{|\vec{b} \times \vec{c}|} \right) = \frac{1}{6} \left| \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \right| \]

Example: If \(\vec{a} = (7,-1,5),\ \vec{b} = (-3,1,0),\ \vec{c} = (3,-1,2)\), find volume.

Scalar triple product:

\[ [\vec{a}\ \vec{b}\ \vec{c}] = \begin{vmatrix} 7 & -1 & 5 \\ -3 & 1 & 0 \\ 3 & -1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = 7(2 - 0) + 1(-6 - 0) + 5(3 - 3) = 14 - 6 = 8 \]

Volume \(= \dfrac{1}{6} |8| = \dfrac{4}{3}\) cubic units.

‚úÖ Final formula: \(\boxed{V = \dfrac{1}{6} \left| \vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} \times \vec{c}) \right|}\)
