üìö Core Definitions

  • Sigma Notation ($\Sigma$): The sum of a series $x_1 + x_2 + \dots + x_n$ is denoted by $\sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$.
  • Sum of First $n$ Natural Numbers ($\Sigma n$): $\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$.
  • Sum of Squares of First $n$ Natural Numbers ($\Sigma n^2$): $\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$.
  • Sum of Cubes of First $n$ Natural Numbers ($\Sigma n^3$): $\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2$.
  • Arithmetico-Geometric Series: A series obtained by multiplying the corresponding terms of an A.P. and a G.P., e.g., $a + (a + d)r + (a + 2d)r^2 + \dots$.
  • Method of Differences: A method to find the sum of a series by expressing it as $f(r+1) - f(r)$ or $f(r) - f(r+1)$.
  • Partial Fractions: A technique to decompose rational expressions into simpler fractions to facilitate summation.
  • Infinite Series: A series with an infinite number of terms, convergent if $|r| < 1$ for geometric series.

Q1. Sum the series $1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + \dots + 10^2$.

Solution:

Using the formula for the sum of squares of first $n$ natural numbers:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

For $n = 10$:

$\sum_{k=1}^{10} k^2 = \frac{10(10+1)(2 \times 10 + 1)}{6} = \frac{10 \times 11 \times 21}{6} = 385$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{385}$

Q2. Sum the series $1^3 + 2^3 + 3^3 + \dots + 15^3$.

Solution:

Using the formula for the sum of cubes of first $n$ natural numbers:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2$

For $n = 15$:

$\sum_{k=1}^{15} k^3 = \left[\frac{15(15+1)}{2}\right]^2 = \left[\frac{15 \times 16}{2}\right]^2 = 120^2 = 14400$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{14400}$

Q3. Sum the series $1^2 + 3^2 + 5^2 + \dots$ up to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The series is the sum of squares of first $n$ odd numbers.

The $k$th odd number is $2k - 1$.

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (2k - 1)^2 = \sum_{k=1}^{n} (4k^2 - 4k + 1) = 4 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 - 4 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k + \sum_{k=1}^{n} 1$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} 1 = n$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (2k - 1)^2 = 4 \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}\right) - 4 \left(\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right) + n$

Simplify:

$= \frac{2n(n+1)(2n+1)}{3} - 2n(n+1) + n$

$= \frac{2n(n+1)(2n+1) - 6n(n+1) + 3n}{3}$

$= \frac{n[2(n+1)(2n+1) - 6(n+1) + 3]}{3}$

$= \frac{n[4n^2 + 6n + 2 - 6n - 6 + 3]}{3}$

$= \frac{n(4n^2 - 1)}{3}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n(4n^2 - 1)}{3}}$

Q4. Sum the series $2^2 + 4^2 + 6^2 + 8^2 + \dots$ up to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The series is the sum of squares of first $n$ even numbers.

The $k$th even number is $2k$.

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (2k)^2 = 4 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = 4 \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}\right) = \frac{2n(n+1)(2n+1)}{3}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{2n(n+1)(2n+1)}{3}}$

Q5. Sum the series $1^3 + 3^3 + 5^3 + \dots$ up to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The series is the sum of cubes of first $n$ odd numbers.

The $k$th odd number is $2k - 1$.

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (2k - 1)^3 = \sum_{k=1}^{n} (8k^3 - 12k^2 + 6k - 1)$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} 1 = n$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (2k - 1)^3 = 8 \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2 - 12 \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}\right) + 6 \left(\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right) - n$

Simplify:

$= 2n^2(n+1)^2 - 2n(n+1)(2n+1) + 3n(n+1) - n$

$= n[2n(n+1)^2 - 2(n+1)(2n+1) + 3(n+1) - 1]$

$= n[2n(n^2 + 2n + 1) - 2(2n^2 + 3n + 1) + 3n + 3 - 1]$

$= n[2n^3 + 4n^2 + 2n - 4n^2 - 6n - 2 + 3n + 2]$

$= n[2n^3 - n]$

$= n^2(2n^2 - 1)$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{n^2(2n^2 - 1)}$

Q6. Sum the series $2 \times 1^2 + 5 \times 2^2 + 8 \times 3^2 + \dots$ up to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The general term of the series is $(3k - 1)k^2$.

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (3k - 1)k^2 = 3 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 - \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (3k - 1)k^2 = 3 \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2 - \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

Simplify:

$= \frac{3n^2(n+1)^2}{4} - \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$= \frac{n(n+1)}{12} [9n(n+1) - 2(2n+1)]$

$= \frac{n(n+1)}{12} [9n^2 + 9n - 4n - 2]$

$= \frac{n(n+1)(9n^2 + 5n - 2)}{12}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n(n+1)(9n^2 + 5n - 2)}{12}}$

Q7. Sum the series $1 + (1 + 2) + (1 + 2 + 3) + \dots$ up to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The $k$th term of the series is $\sum_{i=1}^{k} i = \frac{k(k+1)}{2}$.

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{k(k+1)}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{n} (k^2 + k) = \frac{1}{2} \left(\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 + \sum_{k=1}^{n} k\right)$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{k(k+1)}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right)$

Simplify:

$= \frac{n(n+1)}{4} \left(\frac{2n+1}{3} + 1\right)$

$= \frac{n(n+1)}{4} \left(\frac{2n + 4}{3}\right)$

$= \frac{n(n+1)(n + 2)}{6}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n(n+1)(n+2)}{6}}$

Q8. Sum the series $2 + (2 + 5) + (2 + 5 + 8) + \dots$ up to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The $k$th term of the series is $2 + \sum_{i=1}^{k-1} (3i + 2) = 2 + \frac{(k-1)(3k + 4)}{2}$.

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(2 + \frac{(k-1)(3k + 4)}{2}\right) = 2n + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{n} (3k^2 + k - 4)$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(2 + \frac{(k-1)(3k + 4)}{2}\right) = 2n + \frac{1}{2} \left(3 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 + \sum_{k=1}^{n} k - 4n\right)$

Simplify:

$= 2n + \frac{1}{2} \left(3 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + \frac{n(n+1)}{2} - 4n\right)$

$= 2n + \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{2} + \frac{n(n+1)}{2} - 4n\right)$

$= 2n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+2)}{4} - 2n$

$= \frac{n(n+1)(n+1)}{2}$

$= \frac{n(n+1)^2}{2}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n(n+1)^2}{2}}$

Q9. Find the sum of $n$ terms of the series whose $n$th term is:
(i) $3n^2 + n + 1$
(ii) $n^2 + 4n + 1$
(iii) $n^3 + 3n^2 + 2n + 1$

Solution:

(i) For $a_n = 3n^2 + n + 1$:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = 3 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 + \sum_{k=1}^{n} k + \sum_{k=1}^{n} 1$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} 1 = n$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = 3 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + \frac{n(n+1)}{2} + n$

Simplify:

$= \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{2} + \frac{n(n+1)}{2} + n$

$= \frac{n(n+1)(2n+2)}{2} + n$

$= n(n+1)(n+1) + n$

$= n(n+1)^2 + n$

$= n[(n+1)^2 + 1]$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{n[(n+1)^2 + 1]}$

(ii) For $a_n = n^2 + 4n + 1$:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 + 4 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k + \sum_{k=1}^{n} 1$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} 1 = n$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + 4 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)}{2} + n$

Simplify:

$= \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + 2n(n+1) + n$

$= \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1) + 12n(n+1) + 6n}{6}$

$= \frac{n[2n^2 + 3n + 1 + 12n + 12 + 6]}{6}$

$= \frac{n(2n^2 + 15n + 19)}{6}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n(2n^2 + 15n + 19)}{6}}$

(iii) For $a_n = n^3 + 3n^2 + 2n + 1$:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 + 3 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 + 2 \sum_{k=1}^{n} k + \sum_{k=1}^{n} 1$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3 = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} 1 = n$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \left[\frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right]^2 + 3 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + 2 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)}{2} + n$

Simplify:

$= \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{2} + n(n+1) + n$

$= \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1 + 4)}{2} + n$

$= \frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+5)}{2} + n$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n^2(n+1)^2}{4} + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+5)}{2} + n}$

Q10. (i) Find the sum of first forty six natural numbers starting from 10.
(ii) Find the sum of last 12 terms of the series: $1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + 39$.

Solution:

(i) The sum of first forty six natural numbers starting from 10:

$\sum_{k=10}^{55} k = \sum_{k=1}^{55} k - \sum_{k=1}^{9} k$

Using the formula:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=10}^{55} k = \frac{55 \times 56}{2} - \frac{9 \times 10}{2} = 1540 - 45 = 1495$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{1495}$

(ii) The sum of last 12 terms of the series $1 + 2 + 3 + \dots + 39$:

$\sum_{k=28}^{39} k = \sum_{k=1}^{39} k - \sum_{k=1}^{27} k$

Using the formula:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Thus:

$\sum_{k=28}^{39} k = \frac{39 \times 40}{2} - \frac{27 \times 28}{2} = 780 - 378 = 402$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{402}$

Q1. Sum the series $1 \cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 2^2 + 3 \cdot 2^3 + 4 \cdot 2^4 + \dots$ to 50 terms.

Solution:

The series is an arithmetico-geometric series with $a = 1$, $d = 1$, and $r = 2$.

Using the formula for the sum of $n$ terms of an arithmetico-geometric series:

$S_n = \frac{a}{1 - r} + \frac{dr}{(1 - r)^2} - \frac{dr^n}{(1 - r)^2} - \frac{[a + (n - 1)d]r^n}{1 - r}$

For $n = 50$:

$S_{50} = \frac{1}{1 - 2} + \frac{1 \cdot 2}{(1 - 2)^2} - \frac{1 \cdot 2 \cdot 2^{50}}{(1 - 2)^2} - \frac{[1 + 49 \cdot 1]2^{50}}{1 - 2}$

Simplify:

$S_{50} = -1 + \frac{2}{1} - \frac{2^{51}}{1} + 50 \cdot 2^{50}$

$S_{50} = -1 + 2 - 2^{51} + 50 \cdot 2^{50}$

$S_{50} = 1 - 2^{51} + 25 \cdot 2^{51}$

$S_{50} = 1 + 24 \cdot 2^{51}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{1 + 24 \cdot 2^{51}}$

Q2. Sum the series $1 + 2.5 + 3.5^2 + 4.5^3 + \dots$ to 30 terms.

Solution:

The series is an arithmetico-geometric series with $a = 1$, $d = 1$, and $r = 5$.

Using the formula for the sum of $n$ terms of an arithmetico-geometric series:

$S_n = \frac{a}{1 - r} + \frac{dr}{(1 - r)^2} - \frac{dr^n}{(1 - r)^2} - \frac{[a + (n - 1)d]r^n}{1 - r}$

For $n = 30$:

$S_{30} = \frac{1}{1 - 5} + \frac{1 \cdot 5}{(1 - 5)^2} - \frac{1 \cdot 5 \cdot 5^{30}}{(1 - 5)^2} - \frac{[1 + 29 \cdot 1]5^{30}}{1 - 5}$

Simplify:

$S_{30} = -\frac{1}{4} + \frac{5}{16} - \frac{5^{31}}{16} + \frac{30 \cdot 5^{30}}{4}$

$S_{30} = -\frac{1}{4} + \frac{5}{16} - \frac{5^{31}}{16} + \frac{15 \cdot 5^{30}}{2}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{-\frac{1}{4} + \frac{5}{16} - \frac{5^{31}}{16} + \frac{15 \cdot 5^{30}}{2}}$

Q6. Find the sum to infinity of the series: $1 + \frac{4}{3} + \frac{9}{3^2} + \frac{16}{3^3} + \frac{25}{3^4} + \dots$

Solution:

The series is an infinite arithmetico-geometric series with $a = 1$, $d = 2$, and $r = \frac{1}{3}$.

Using the formula for the sum of an infinite arithmetico-geometric series:

$S = \frac{a}{1 - r} + \frac{dr}{(1 - r)^2}$

Thus:

$S = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{1}{3}} + \frac{2 \cdot \frac{1}{3}}{(1 - \frac{1}{3})^2} = \frac{1}{\frac{2}{3}} + \frac{\frac{2}{3}}{\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^2} = \frac{3}{2} + \frac{\frac{2}{3}}{\frac{4}{9}} = \frac{3}{2} + \frac{3}{2} = 3$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{3}$

Q7. Find the sum to infinity of the series: $1 + 9a + 25a^2 + 49a^3 + \dots$ where $|a| < 1$.

Solution:

The series is an infinite arithmetico-geometric series with $a = 1$, $d = 8$, and $r = a$.

Using the formula for the sum of an infinite arithmetico-geometric series:

$S = \frac{a}{1 - r} + \frac{dr}{(1 - r)^2}$

Thus:

$S = \frac{1}{1 - a} + \frac{8a}{(1 - a)^2}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{1}{1 - a} + \frac{8a}{(1 - a)^2}}$

Q10. Find the sum to infinity of the series: $1 + \frac{5}{3} + \frac{12}{3^2} + \frac{22}{3^3} + \frac{35}{3^4} + \dots$

Solution:

The series is an infinite arithmetico-geometric series with $a = 1$, $d = 3$, and $r = \frac{1}{3}$.

Using the formula for the sum of an infinite arithmetico-geometric series:

$S = \frac{a}{1 - r} + \frac{dr}{(1 - r)^2}$

Thus:

$S = \frac{1}{1 - \frac{1}{3}} + \frac{3 \cdot \frac{1}{3}}{(1 - \frac{1}{3})^2} = \frac{1}{\frac{2}{3}} + \frac{1}{\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^2} = \frac{3}{2} + \frac{9}{4} = \frac{6}{4} + \frac{9}{4} = \frac{15}{4}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{15}{4}}$

Q1. Find the sum of the series: $3 + 6 + 12 + 21 + 33 + \dots$ to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The differences of consecutive terms are in A.P.: $3, 6, 9, 12, \dots$

Using the method of differences:

Let $S_n = 3 + 6 + 12 + 21 + 33 + \dots + a_n$

Then, $S_n = \pm 3 \pm 6 \pm 12 \pm 21 \pm \dots \pm a_n$

Subtracting:

$0 = 3 + \{3 + 6 + 9 + \dots \text{ to } (n-1) \text{ terms}\} - a_n$

Thus:

$a_n = 3 + \frac{(n-1)}{2} [2 \times 3 + (n-2) \times 3] = 3 + \frac{3(n-1)(n)}{2}$

Now, the sum:

$S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(3 + \frac{3(k-1)k}{2}\right) = 3n + \frac{3}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{n} (k^2 - k)$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Thus:

$S_n = 3n + \frac{3}{2} \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} - \frac{n(n+1)}{2}\right)$

Simplify:

$= 3n + \frac{3}{2} \left(\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1) - 3n(n+1)}{6}\right)$

$= 3n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n-2)}{4}$

$= 3n + \frac{n(n+1)(n-1)}{2}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{3n + \frac{n(n+1)(n-1)}{2}}$

Q2. Find the sum of the series: $5 + 10 + 17 + 26 + 37 + \dots$ to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The differences of consecutive terms are in A.P.: $5, 7, 9, 11, \dots$

Using the method of differences:

Let $S_n = 5 + 10 + 17 + 26 + 37 + \dots + a_n$

Then, $S_n = \pm 5 \pm 10 \pm 17 \pm 26 \pm \dots \pm a_n$

Subtracting:

$0 = 5 + \{5 + 7 + 9 + \dots \text{ to } (n-1) \text{ terms}\} - a_n$

Thus:

$a_n = 5 + \frac{(n-1)}{2} [2 \times 5 + (n-2) \times 2] = 5 + \frac{(n-1)(2n + 6)}{2} = 5 + (n-1)(n+3)$

Now, the sum:

$S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \sum_{k=1}^{n} [5 + (k-1)(k+3)] = 5n + \sum_{k=1}^{n} (k^2 + 2k - 3)$

Using the formulas:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^2 = \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Thus:

$S_n = 5n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + 2 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)}{2} - 3n$

Simplify:

$= 5n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} + n(n+1) - 3n$

$= 2n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1 + 6)}{6}$

$= 2n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+7)}{6}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{2n + \frac{n(n+1)(2n+7)}{6}}$

Q4. Find the sum of the series: $3 + 6 + 15 + 42 + 123 + \dots$ to $n$ terms.

Solution:

The differences of consecutive terms are in G.P.: $3, 9, 27, 81, \dots$

Using the method of differences:

Let $S_n = 3 + 6 + 15 + 42 + 123 + \dots + a_n$

Then, $S_n = \pm 3 \pm 6 \pm 15 \pm 42 \pm \dots \pm a_n$

Subtracting:

$0 = 3 + \{3 + 9 + 27 + \dots \text{ to } (n-1) \text{ terms}\} - a_n$

Thus:

$a_n = 3 + \frac{3(3^{n-1} - 1)}{3 - 1} = 3 + \frac{3^{n} - 3}{2}$

Now, the sum:

$S_n = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(3 + \frac{3^{k} - 3}{2}\right) = 3n + \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{n} 3^{k} - \frac{3n}{2}$

Using the formula for the sum of a geometric series:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} 3^{k} = \frac{3(3^{n} - 1)}{3 - 1} = \frac{3^{n+1} - 3}{2}$

Thus:

$S_n = 3n + \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{3^{n+1} - 3}{2} - \frac{3n}{2} = \frac{3n}{2} + \frac{3^{n+1} - 3}{4}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{3n}{2} + \frac{3^{n+1} - 3}{4}}$

Q1. Find the sum of the series: $\frac{1}{1 \cdot 3} + \frac{1}{3 \cdot 5} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 7} + \dots$ to $n$ terms.

Solution:

Using partial fractions:

$\frac{1}{(2k-1)(2k+1)} = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{2k-1} - \frac{1}{2k+1}\right)$

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{(2k-1)(2k+1)} = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(\frac{1}{2k-1} - \frac{1}{2k+1}\right)$

This is a telescoping series:

$\frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{1} - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5} + \dots + \frac{1}{2n-1} - \frac{1}{2n+1}\right) = \frac{1}{2} \left(1 - \frac{1}{2n+1}\right)$

Simplify:

$= \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2n}{2n+1} = \frac{n}{2n+1}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n}{2n+1}}$

Q2. Find the sum of the series: $\frac{1}{1 \cdot 5} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 9} + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 13} + \dots$ to $n$ terms.

Solution:

Using partial fractions:

$\frac{1}{(4k-3)(4k+1)} = \frac{1}{4} \left(\frac{1}{4k-3} - \frac{1}{4k+1}\right)$

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{(4k-3)(4k+1)} = \frac{1}{4} \sum_{k=1}^{n} \left(\frac{1}{4k-3} - \frac{1}{4k+1}\right)$

This is a telescoping series:

$\frac{1}{4} \left(\frac{1}{1} - \frac{1}{5} + \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{9} + \dots + \frac{1}{4n-3} - \frac{1}{4n+1}\right) = \frac{1}{4} \left(1 - \frac{1}{4n+1}\right)$

Simplify:

$= \frac{1}{4} \cdot \frac{4n}{4n+1} = \frac{n}{4n+1}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{n}{4n+1}}$

Q3. Find the sum of the series: $\frac{1}{1 \cdot 5 \cdot 9} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 9 \cdot 13} + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 13 \cdot 17} + \dots$ to infinity.

Solution:

Using partial fractions:

$\frac{1}{(4k-3)(4k+1)(4k+5)} = \frac{1}{16} \left(\frac{1}{(4k-3)(4k+1)} - \frac{1}{(4k+1)(4k+5)}\right)$

Further decompose:

$\frac{1}{(4k-3)(4k+1)} = \frac{1}{4} \left(\frac{1}{4k-3} - \frac{1}{4k+1}\right)$

$\frac{1}{(4k+1)(4k+5)} = \frac{1}{4} \left(\frac{1}{4k+1} - \frac{1}{4k+5}\right)$

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(4k-3)(4k+1)(4k+5)} = \frac{1}{16} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{4k-3} - \frac{1}{4k+5}\right)$

This is a telescoping series:

$\frac{1}{16} \left(\frac{1}{1} - \frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{13} + \dots\right)$

The sum converges to:

$\frac{1}{16} \left(\frac{1}{1} + \frac{1}{5}\right) = \frac{1}{16} \cdot \frac{6}{5} = \frac{3}{40}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{3}{40}}$

Q5. Find the sum of the series: $\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{4k^2 - 1}$.

Solution:

Using partial fractions:

$\frac{1}{4k^2 - 1} = \frac{1}{(2k-1)(2k+1)} = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{2k-1} - \frac{1}{2k+1}\right)$

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{4k^2 - 1} = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{2k-1} - \frac{1}{2k+1}\right)$

This is a telescoping series:

$\frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{1}{1} - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{5} + \dots\right) = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{1} = \frac{1}{2}$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{\frac{1}{2}}$

Q7. Find the sum of the series: $\sum_{k=2}^{\infty} \frac{1}{(k^2 - k)}$.

Solution:

Using partial fractions:

$\frac{1}{k^2 - k} = \frac{1}{k(k-1)} = \frac{1}{k-1} - \frac{1}{k}$

Thus, the sum is:

$\sum_{k=2}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k^2 - k} = \sum_{k=2}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{k-1} - \frac{1}{k}\right)$

This is a telescoping series:

$\left(\frac{1}{1} - \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} + \dots\right) = 1$

‚úÖ Answer: $\boxed{1}$
